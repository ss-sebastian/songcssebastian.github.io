I am a master's student working on computational modelling in language and cognition. My current research uses neural networks and representational analyses to connect artificial model behaviour with human behavioural and neural data. I am especially interested in benchmarking models against human data, LLM-based representational similarity analysis, and RNN modelling of behavioural and fMRI responses. More broadly, I want to study how computational models can explain human language, behaviour, and brain responses without overstating what those models capture.

## Current Focus

- Developing a computational modelling profile at the intersection of language, cognition, and neuroimaging
- Evaluating models in terms of human-model alignment rather than task accuracy alone
- Linking behavioural variation, representational structure, and neural signals in a common modelling framework

## Featured Projects

### 1. Benchmarking Human-Model Alignment

I work on benchmarking computational models against human behavioural data, with a focus on whether models explain participant-level behavioural patterns rather than only producing strong aggregate performance. This includes trial-level variance, response-time prediction, participant-level model selection, and comparison with cognitive baselines where appropriate.

### 2. LLM RSA for Multilingual Semantic Representation

I use representational similarity analysis to examine whether multilingual language models recover cross-linguistic semantic structure. This work focuses on layer-wise representations, language-pair comparisons, form-versus-meaning controls, and the broader question of whether model representations align with multilingual semantic geometry.

### 3. RNN Modelling of Behavioural and fMRI Data

I am building recurrent neural network models for auditory perception, predictive coding, and sequence decision-making tasks. The goal is to model online sequence processing, derive trial-level response-time readouts, analyze hidden-state dynamics, and test how model states align with behavioural and neural responses.
