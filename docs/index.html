Research Focus

I am building a computational modelling profile at the intersection of language, cognition, and neuroimaging. Across projects, I am interested in whether artificial models can account for participant-level behaviour, response-time structure, and brain-relevant representations, rather than only producing strong aggregate task accuracy.

Current Direction

  • Benchmarking computational models against human behavioural variation
  • Representational similarity analysis with multilingual language models
  • RNN modelling of auditory sequence processing, response time, and fMRI responses

Working Style

  • Developing reproducible analysis and modelling pipelines in Python and R
  • Combining behavioural data, representational analysis, and neural modelling
  • Treating LLMs and RNNs as computational models for cognitive questions

Current Modelling Projects

Benchmarking Human-Model Alignment

I am developing benchmarking pipelines that evaluate whether computational models explain human behavioural variation rather than only final accuracy. This includes participant-level model selection, trial-level variance, response-time prediction, and comparisons with cognitive baselines where appropriate.

LLM RSA for Multilingual Semantic Representation

This project uses representational similarity analysis to test whether multilingual language models recover cross-linguistic semantic structure. I am especially interested in layer-wise representations, language-pair comparisons, form-versus-meaning controls, and the geometry of semantic spaces across languages.

RNN Modelling of Behavioural and fMRI Data

I am building recurrent neural network models for auditory perception, predictive coding, and sequence decision-making tasks. The broader goal is to model online sequence processing, derive trial-level RT readouts, inspect hidden-state dynamics, and evaluate alignment with behavioural and neural data.
